# ðŸ› ï¸ What I Actually Did

This simulation wasn't just about writing code; it was about solving problems. Here is the breakdown of my four-stage process:

1. Data Detective Work (EDA)

Before building any models, I had to understand what was going on inside the dataset. I spent time cleaning the data and running an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).

What I found: There was a clear pattern. Customers who had been employed for less than two years and had high credit utilization (using >80% of their limit) were significantly more likely to miss payments.

2. Building the Prediction Model

Instead of manually coding a complex machine learning model from scratch, I proposed a No-Code Predictive Framework.

The Approach: I used Generative AI to help write the logic for a decision tree. This allowed me to quickly categorize customers into "High," "Medium," and "Low" risk groups based on the indicators I found in step one. It showed how GenAI can speed up the technical heavy lifting.

3. Translating Code to Business Strategy

Data is useless if the CEO doesn't understand it. I took my technical findings and turned them into a clear, jargon-free presentation for senior leadership.

The Pitch: I explained that by targeting the specific risk factors I found, we could predictâ€”and potentially preventâ€”about 30-40% of future delinquencies just by intervening earlier.

4. Designing the AI Agent

The final piece was the solution. I designed an AI-Driven Collections Strategy.

How it works: Instead of overwhelmed call center agents calling everyone, an AI "agent" handles the initial outreach for low-risk late payers. It creates a smoother, less aggressive experience for customers while freeing up human staff to handle the complicated, high-risk cases.
